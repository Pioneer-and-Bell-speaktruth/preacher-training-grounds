▶ Understanding the Structure of the Bible

Before beginning a one-year study plan, it helps to understand how the Bible is organized. These sections will guide your reading and deepen your study.

Old Testament Subsections

The Law (Pentateuch)

Genesis through Deuteronomy — creation, patriarchs, Exodus, and the giving of the Law.

Historical Books

Joshua through Esther — Judges, United Kingdom, Divided Kingdom.

Wisdom & Poetry

Job, Psalms, Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, Song of Solomon.

Major Prophets

Isaiah through Daniel.

Minor Prophets

Hosea through Malachi.

New Testament Subsections

The Life and Preaching of Christ

Matthew, Mark, Luke, John.

Church History

Acts.

The Epistles

Romans through Jude.

Prophecy / Apocalyptic

Revelation.

▶ How This One-Year Study Plan Works

Yearly Structure

• Old Testament + New Testament each day

• Psalms and Proverbs woven throughout

• Chronological flow where helpful

Study Method

• Observe — What does the text say?

• Interpret — What does it mean in the original language (not what you 'think' it means)?

• Apply — How should I live this?

Weekly Rhythm

• 6 days of reading and study

• 1 day of reflection
