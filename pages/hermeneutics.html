Hermeneutics Basics

What Is Hermeneutics?

Hermeneutics is the discipline of interpreting Scripture faithfully— understanding what God meant before we ask what it means for us.

Key Principles

• Context: literary, historical, and biblical.

• Genre: narrative, poetry, epistle, prophecy, etc.

• Word Studies: careful, not speculative.

• Cross-References: Scripture interprets Scripture.

• Authorial Audience: who is the author speaking to or about.

• Authority of Text: command, example, necessary inference.

Checklist

□ Have I read the passage in context?

□ Do I understand the genre?

□ Am I letting Scripture speak, not forcing my ideas?

□ Is my application rooted in the text?
